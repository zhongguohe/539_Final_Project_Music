Icons of Mandopop

Meet the legendary voices shaping Chinese pop music across generations.

Jay Chou

周杰伦

The King of Mandopop. Pioneer of blending Chinese melodies with Western R&B and hip-hop.

Wang Leehom

王力宏

A pioneer of East-meets-West fusion, blending Chinese traditional sounds with pop, hip-hop, and R&B.

JJ Lin

林俊杰

Singapore-born vocal powerhouse known for emotional ballads and innovative pop arrangements.

David Tao

陶喆

The godfather of Chinese R&B. Brought soulful grooves and jazz-influenced songwriting to Mandopop.

G.E.M.

邓紫棋

A powerhouse vocalist from Hong Kong who rose to fame with explosive live performances.

Jason Zhang

张杰

Known for his powerful high notes and energetic stage presence. A staple of modern Chinese pop.

Khalil Fong

方大同

A soulful singer-songwriter blending R&B, neo-soul, and jazz into the Mandopop landscape.

Eason Chan

陈奕迅

A Hong Kong icon beloved for deeply emotional ballads and versatile vocal range.

Stefanie Sun

孙燕姿

Singapore's sweetheart whose warm voice defined early 2000s Mandopop for a generation.

Xu Song (Vae)

许嵩

The king of Chinese internet music — writes, produces, and sings all his own songs.

Silence Wang

汪苏泷

Internet Big Three member whose catchy songs dominated campus culture and KTV rooms.

Xu Liang

徐良

Known for quirky, energetic duets that became anthems of early 2010s Chinese internet pop.

Mayday

五月天

Taiwan's biggest rock band — pop-rock anthems about youth, dreams, and perseverance.

Li Ronghao

李荣浩

Singer-songwriter and producer whose warm, smoky voice defines modern mainland Chinese pop.

Joker Xue

薛之谦

From underdog to megastar — his comeback hit "Actor" defined a generation of Chinese ballads.

MC HotDog

热狗

A founding father of Chinese-language rap, known for raw, satirical lyrics about everyday life.

Wilber Pan

潘玮柏

Chinese-American rapper who brought hip-hop swagger into mainstream Mandopop.

GAI

GAI周延

Chongqing rapper who won The Rap of China, fusing dialect, tradition, and hard-hitting flows.

Explore by Genre

Chinese pop music spans many styles — from heartfelt ballads to hard-hitting hip-hop.

Mandarin Ballads

Emotional, melody-driven love songs — the heart and soul of Mandopop.

Chinese R&B

Smooth grooves and soulful vocals — pioneered by David Tao and Khalil Fong.

Chinese Hip-Hop

Rapid-fire lyrics and hard beats — Jay Chou and a new generation lead the way.

Chinese Rock & Pop-Rock

Guitar-driven anthems with emotional depth from artists like Mayday and Beyond.
